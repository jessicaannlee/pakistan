PAKISTAN: DRONE CASUALTIES

"Remember that the terrorists we are after target civilians, and the death toll from their acts of terrorism against Muslims dwarfs any estimate of civilian casualties from drone strikes."
-- President Obama, 23 May 2013

But what happens when we're wrong? This map examines locations in Pakistan that reported civilian and children deaths from drone strikes. Among the locations here,

      Norak, Pakistan (2011)
      Total casualties (minimum reported): 2
      Civilian casualties (minimum reported): 2
      Children casualties (minimum reported): 2.

While the figures on hand are estimates --- being that there remain wide gaps between what the U.S. government claims and what nongovernmental parties assert --- the data is no less frightening to contemplate. From above, it appears that children were the sole victims of a 2011 CIA drone strike.

The last child victim to a drone strike was in 2012 in Hamzoni, according to what the London Bureau of Investigative Journalism has made available. The marked locations are scaled by the number of total casualties. Click on the markers to get more information.

Information was collected from a BIJ article, "Get the data: What the drones strike". BIJ has reported extensively on U.S. drone strikes: Where the Drones Strike.
